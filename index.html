Bob Dylan

The Singer, The Songwriter, The Poet

A short overview over Bob Dylan's life

  • Born on May 24th 1941 as Robert Allen Zimmermann
  • January 1961: Coming to Greenwich Village, NYC and getting involved in the local folk music scene
  • 1962: Release of his first, self-titled Album
  • 1963: "The Freewheelin' Bob Dylan" is released. It is the breakthrough for Bob's career
  • 1964: "The Times They Are a-Changin' is released. Bob becomes the "voice of his generation"
  • 1965: Bob Dylan plays the electric guitar on the Newport Folk Festival and baffles the crowd. They call him a traitor and boo him off the stage. In the coming months he would change from folk music to rock and create some of the most famous and widely acclaimed albums of all time.
  • 1966: Bob Dylan retreats from the public for two years after a motorcycle accident.
  • 1975: Bob Dylan starts a come-back with his Rolling Thunder Revue, which is sold out in hours. His coming releases show him experimenting with different styles and genres.
  • 1980s: Bob Dylan becomes christian and starts releasing gospel-like songs and albums.
  • 1988: Bob Dylan starts his Neverending Tour, which continues to this day.
  • since 1994: The 90s marked Bobs Comeback with the start being his gig at MTV unplugged. From then on, he released some of his best and most interesting albums so far and still goes on tour regularly. He will turn 80 on May 24 2021.
